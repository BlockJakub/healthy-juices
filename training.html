Training

Guidance based on AHA, CDC, and WHO recommendations

Introduction

Regular physical activity strengthens the heart, muscles, bones, and mind. It supports healthy weight, improves insulin sensitivity, lowers blood pressure, and enhances mood and sleep. Training plans should be gradual, enjoyable, and sustainable.

Weekly Activity Targets

  • Adults
  • Aerobic: 150–300 minutes/week of moderate intensity (e.g., brisk walking) or 75–150 minutes/week vigorous (e.g., running) (CDC), (WHO).
  • Muscle‑strengthening: ≥2 days/week covering major muscle groups (e.g., resistance training) (AHA).
  • Reduce sitting: break up prolonged sedentary time with brief movement bouts.

Training Mix

  • Cardio: walking, cycling, swimming, dancing.
  • Strength: bodyweight, free weights, machines, or resistance bands.
  • Mobility: dynamic warm‑ups, stretching, yoga, or Pilates.
  • Progress: increase volume or intensity gradually (5–10%/week) and include rest days.
Safety & Consistency
  • Warm up 5–10 minutes; cool down and stretch after sessions.
  • Hydrate; fuel with balanced meals to support training load.
  • Listen to your body—adjust for pain, illness, or fatigue; seek medical advice if needed.
  • Pair activity with healthy sleep and nutrition for best results.

References

  • American Heart Association – Fitness & Activity
  • CDC – Physical Activity Basics
  • WHO – Physical Activity Fact Sheet
